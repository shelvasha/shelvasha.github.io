My name is Shelvasha (please call me Shel) and I am a postdoc researcher in the Department of Bioinformatics at UNC Charlotte.

I'm part of the Cooper Lab, led by Dr. Elizabeth Cooper and we are based out of the Kannapolis Research Campus in Kannapolis, NC. Currently, our primarily research focus is on plant evolution and genomics, in particular, the evolution of sugar accumulation in S. bicolor (Sorghum) and proposed evolutionary origins of domesticated Sorghum. Other cool topics we are investigating include Wyeomyia smithii, a uniquely adapted mosquito species that lays its eggs inside of the carnivorous purple pitcher plant, and Culex tarsalis, another species of mosquito known as carriers of West Nile and Zika viruses.

Aside from my research, I'm also a photography enthusiast with a thing for music and food.
