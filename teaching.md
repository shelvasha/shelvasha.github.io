### Courses

#### 2017-2019

**BINF 3201: Genomic Methods**

*   Lecture topics introduce students to core concepts in genomics that allow bench scientists to acquire large datasets in a high-throughput manner as well as address the computational methods used to analyze these data resources.

*   Laboratory component of the Genomics Methods course gave students hands-on experience in setting up and performing experiments with an emphasis on nucleic acid and protein profiling, understanding and trouble-shooting published protocols, and interpreting the data using computational tools.

*   Teaching assistant for three years as part of the [GAANN Fellowship](https://www2.ed.gov/programs/gaann/index.html).

#### 2016

**BINF 3101: Sequence Analysis**

*   The purpose, application, and biological significance of bioinformatics methods that identify sequence similarity, methods that rely on sequence similarity to produce models of biological processes and systems, as well as methods that use sequence characteristics to predict functional features in genomic sequence data.

*   Laboratory component of the Sequence Analysis provided a hands-on computing lab in which students learn similarity methods.

*   Teaching assistant for the 2016 Fall semester prior to beginning Bioinformatics PhD program.
